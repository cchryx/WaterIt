Build Introduction

Note that all of the parts we used in our build is recycled and reused to be placed in our product, keeping an evironmental friendly image. Our build is made with two "+" shaped supports connected by 2 metal beams to hold them together. Next we have a circlular ring attached to the top, holding a cup shaped piece with a hole on the bottom. We then cut off the top of a water bottle and placed in the cup shaped piece, which wil serve as the water source. Finally we will have some tubing, which one end is connected to the lid of the water bottle through the hole on the cup shaped piece. The other end is attached to the servo and as the water source is higher than the end on the servo, the water will flow downwards and directed to the plant.


The circuit parts of our product include the following:

Micro:bit - The Micro:bit is the brain of our product. The Micro:bit decides when to water the plant from the measurement of the Sensor:bit.

Sensor:bit - The Sensor:bit measures the conductivity of the soil. The soil becomes conductive when there is more moisture.

2 X AAA Battery - We power the Micro:bit using 2 AAA batteries which provide a total of 3V of power (1.5V per one).

4 X AA Battery - We power the servo motor with 4 AA batteries which provide a total of 6V of power (1.5V per one).

Servos Motor - The servo motor consumes 4-6v of power. We connected it to another battery for it to work as the Micro:bit could only output 3V.

Wires - Jumper and normal wires to connect all of our components in a circuit.

Circuit Diagram of Smart Plant Managing Device

Our product consists of four main components as displayed in this circuit. The Mirco:bit is the brain of the system and is connected to the servo and the moisture sensor. The Micro Bit reads moisture levels from the moisture sensor to determine when the plant needed to be watered, and it will turn the servo accordingly. The sensor uses Pin 1 and Pin 2 of the Micro Bit while the servo uses Pin 16. The Micro Bit is powered by a 9 volt battery pack and the servo is powered by a 6 volt battery pack.
