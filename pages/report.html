Finding Materials Needed

Contributors: Everyone
Completed on June 05
Finding the materials we needed for our product was a challenge as the first idea involved a whole enclosed system. It took us basically everyday to find the right materials that were needed.
June 01: We had the alligator clips and nails + our Micro:bit
June 02: Everyone actively tried to come up with ideas of what we needed in the original design of our product which was enclosed. This involved thinking of buying two clear boxes and a slider plus a spring which we can cut out along with 4 slotted angles to support the structure. We did some research on the materials, found out that the original design had too many flaws and decided to come up with a new design.
June 03: Chris and William found slotted angles in the scrap room and decided to use it in our build. At this point we thought of a new design that was much more simple involving turing piping to stop the water from flowing as well as letting it flow (credits to Mr. Lu for helping us find this new solution and also providing the tubing), so we got wires. William also brought 2 flower pots from home to use in our build. We also got a plastic water bottle that would work as the water supply for the watering system.
June 04: Michael found a slotted angle that was the exact height as one that was previously found. Along with that, we found some 3D prints that we could use for the base and covering. Jason also asked for a Sensor:bit and servos motor as we needed some more pins to control the watering system.
June 05: We got the 3V battery that was required for powering the Micro:bit as well as the 9V battery to power the servos which required 4.5-6V. The battery had only like 7V so it worked fine. Mr. Lu also found us a 3D print circle and a cone shaped funnel which we used to hold the water bottle.

Test The Prototype

Completed June 06
Contributors: Everyone
June 01: We got the moisture sensor and Micro:bit to test the moisture sensor part of our build. We went outside to get a video recording, however we had trouble recording the information presented by the LEDs because the sun was bright. We stuck it into the earth and confirmed that it worked.
June 03: We got tubing and a plastic water bottle to test how the water will drip into the plant. Made a mess with water everywhere, but had a plan of how it would work.
June 04: We got skeptical that the moisture sensor wasn’t working so we decided to test on dried paper towels. We kept getting 1023 which was the max value and it didn’t make sense, however we found errors in the code and fixed it. We also learned that connectivity issues could also result in the wrong readings so we had to be careful how we wire managed.
June 05-06: We had trouble with the servos and deciding where to put it with consideration of the moisture sensor as well. We ended up using pins 1 and 2 for the moisture sensor and then pin 16 for the servos. We tested it with a small servo, however it was not strong enough and so we needed a bigger and stronger one. But there were challenges with needing it to be connected to an external battery which we figured out on the last day of testing our prototype.

Test Our Build

Completed June 15
Contributors: Everyone
June 14: After we attached everything on our build, we had to test everything to see if it works. First, before testing the actual parts of our build, we found out that when the water bottle is on our design, one side gets too heavy and will tip over if the water bottle is full. We decided to get open the water bottle and not fill it all the way so one side won’t be too heavy. After we got that figured out, we had to test to see if our moisture detector works and it did. Next, we checked the servos, turns out the servos wouldn’t turn even after plugging in everything. We ended up not figuring out the reason for it on this day.
June 15: Chris and William got Mr. Lu to help us with testing the servo and it turns out that the servos were fine, so we experimented a bit more, switching wires, soldering more wires and finally, after we switched the 6V battery for the 9V battery, the servo started to work. With that, we have finished testing everything and our build is working fine.
